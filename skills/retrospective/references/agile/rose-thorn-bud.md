# Rose-Thorn-Bud

<Metadata>
Name: Rose-Thorn-Bud
Category: agile
Aliases: Rose/Bud/Thorn, Garden Retrospective, RBT
Duration: 30-45 minutes
Team Size: 3-8 (max 12)
Best For: Balancing reflection with forward-looking opportunity identification using an approachable nature metaphor
Definition: A nature/garden metaphor retrospective that categorizes experiences as Roses (positives/highlights), Thorns (challenges/pain points), and Buds (potential/opportunities for growth), with the "Bud" category explicitly asking about emerging opportunities rather than just problems.
</Metadata>

<Gotchas>
1. Use when: Want to balance reflection with forward-looking opportunity identification; design thinking or creative environments; team needs a gentler format than "What Went Wrong"; new teams building trust; want to identify and nurture emerging ideas
2. Do NOT use when: Deep-dive needed on systemic failures; highly technical root-cause analysis required; team in crisis needing urgent action; metaphors feel patronizing to the audience
3. Buds as rehashed problems: "Bud: we could stop having so many meetings" is a Thorn in disguise. Fix: Buds should be genuine new opportunities. Ask "What if we tried async standups?"
4. Skipping Buds: Rushing past the most unique category to get to action items from Thorns. Fix: Protect Bud timebox. This is where innovation lives.
5. No Roses: Team jumps straight to problems. Fix: Insist on starting with celebrations. It builds psychological safety for honest Thorns.
6. Buds never revisited: Identified opportunities never followed up in future sprints. Fix: Revisit last retro's Buds at the start of the next one. Ask "Did any buds bloom?"
7. Too many Thorns: Board becomes overwhelmingly negative. Fix: Balance by requiring equal contribution to all three categories.
</Gotchas>

<Instructions>
<Workflow - Rose-Thorn-Bud
  description="Facilitation guide for Rose-Thorn-Bud retrospective."
>
1. [Setup] Prepare a board with three sections labeled "Rose 🌹," "Thorn 🌵," and "Bud 🌱." Use pink/red, gray/brown, and green sticky notes respectively. Allocate time: 7 min brainstorm, 10 min sharing, 10 min discussion, 8 min actions, 5 min close.
2. [Open] (3 minutes)
   - Read the Prime Directive
   - Frame: "Think of our sprint as a garden. What bloomed beautifully? What was prickly? And what's just starting to sprout, new ideas or opportunities that could grow with attention?"
   - Clarify: "Buds are NOT problems reframed. They're genuine new opportunities or emerging ideas."
3. [Stage: Silent Reflection] (7 minutes)
   - Purpose: Generate items across all three categories
   - Prompt: "ROSE: What bloomed this sprint? What are you proud of, grateful for, or celebrating? THORN: What was prickly? What caused pain, frustration, or difficulty? BUD: What's just starting to sprout? What new ideas, opportunities, or experiments could grow if we nurture them?"
   - Facilitation tip: Remind people that Buds are forward-looking. Something they spotted that has potential, not a problem to solve.
4. [Stage: Share Roses] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Start with celebration to build positive energy
   - Prompt: "Let's start with what bloomed. Share your roses. What went well?"
   - Facilitation tip: Celebrate genuinely. Ask follow-up: "What conditions helped this bloom? Can we create more of those conditions?"
5. [Stage: Share Thorns] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Acknowledge pain points honestly
   - Prompt: "Now the prickly parts. What thorns did you encounter? What caused frustration or difficulty?"
   - Facilitation tip: Listen without immediately jumping to solutions. Ask "What's at the root of this thorn?"
6. [Stage: Share Buds] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Surface emerging opportunities and nascent ideas
   - Prompt: "What buds did you notice? What's just starting to sprout that could become something great with attention? What experiments or new approaches are emerging?"
   - Facilitation tip: This is the most unique and valuable stage. Don't rush it. Buds are where innovation lives.
7. [Stage: Prioritize & Decide] (8 minutes)
   - Purpose: Choose which thorns to address and which buds to nurture
   - Prompt: "Vote: Which thorn needs the most urgent attention? Which bud has the most growth potential?"
   - Facilitation tip: Select 1-2 thorns to address AND 1-2 buds to nurture. Balance fixing with growing.
8. [Synthesize] Create action items that address top thorns AND invest in top buds. Document roses for team celebration.
9. [Close] (3 minutes)
   - Read action items
   - Ask: "What do we want to water (nurture) next sprint?"
   - Thank the team for tending the garden
</Workflow - Rose-Thorn-Bud>
</Instructions>

<Resources>
- Start with Roses ALWAYS. It builds the psychological safety needed for honest Thorns
- Buds are where innovation lives. Encourage wild ideas. "What if we tried..." is a perfect Bud starter
- For remote teams, use green/pink/yellow virtual sticky notes to maintain the visual garden feel
- Revisit last retro's Buds at the start of the next one: "Did any buds bloom?"
- This format works beautifully for design sprints, hackathon retros, and creative team reflections
- The metaphor naturally de-escalates tension. "Thorn" feels less confrontational than "failure"
</Resources>
