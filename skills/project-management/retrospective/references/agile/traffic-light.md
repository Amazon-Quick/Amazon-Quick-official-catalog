# Traffic Light Retrospective

<Metadata>
Name: Traffic Light Retrospective
Category: agile
Aliases: Red-Yellow-Green, Stoplight Retro, RAG Retrospective
Duration: 20-35 minutes
Team Size: 3-10 (max 15)
Best For: Universal, visual format that transcends language barriers - zero explanation needed, immediate engagement
Definition: A simple visual format using traffic light colors to categorize team actions and practices (Green for start/go, Yellow for continue/caution, and Red for stop), using the universal recognition of traffic signals for immediate intuitive understanding across cultures.
</Metadata>

<Gotchas>
1. Use when: Need a universally understood format with zero learning curve; cross-cultural or multilingual teams; quick, visual check-in; Start-Stop-Continue needs a visual refresh; non-technical teams or large mixed groups
2. Do NOT use when: Need nuanced categories beyond three (too similar to Start-Stop-Continue); deep analysis required; team has color-blind members without accommodations (accessibility concern); need forward-looking elements (risks, experiments)
3. Accessibility failure: Using only colors without text labels excludes color-blind team members (~8% of men). Fix: ALWAYS add text labels alongside colors. This is non-negotiable.
4. Conflating with RAG status: This is about practices to start/continue/stop, NOT project health indicators. Fix: Clarify the distinction upfront if the team uses RAG reporting elsewhere.
5. All Yellow: Team plays it safe, and everything is "fine, continue." Fix: Challenge. Ask "Nothing to start or stop? Really?"
6. Red without specifics: "Stop: bad communication." Fix: Push for specifics, such as "Stop: 45-minute standups."
7. Just a themed Start-Stop-Continue: If you're not using the VISUAL nature (colors, positioning), just use SSC. Fix: Use actual colored cards/stickies/circles. The visual is the value.
</Gotchas>

<Instructions>
<Workflow - Traffic Light Retrospective
  description="Facilitation guide for Traffic Light retrospective."
>
1. [Setup] Prepare a board with three large colored circles or columns: Green 🟢, Yellow 🟡, Red 🔴. Use matching colored sticky notes. Add text labels for accessibility: "GO (Start)," "CAUTION (Continue)," "STOP." Allocate time: 5 min brainstorm, 5 min share, 10 min discuss, 5 min actions, 3 min close.
2. [Open] (2 minutes)
   - Read the Prime Directive
   - Frame: "Everyone knows traffic lights. GREEN = Go! New things to start. YELLOW = Caution, keep going, these are working. RED = Stop. Things to halt."
   - Note: Always add text labels alongside colors for accessibility (color-blindness).
3. [Stage: Silent Brainstorm] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Categorize practices and ideas by traffic light signal
   - Prompt: "GREEN 🟢: What new ideas or practices should we GO with? Give them the green light! YELLOW 🟡: What's working well, keep rolling, proceed with caution, don't change? RED 🔴: What should we STOP? Full stop. Hit the brakes."
   - Facilitation tip: The "green light" framing for new ideas creates positive energy around starting things.
4. [Stage: Share & Place] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Visualize the team's collective traffic signals
   - Prompt: "Place your items under the right light. Brief one-liner as you go."
   - Facilitation tip: For large groups (10+), have people place silently then do a read-through together.
5. [Stage: Discuss Patterns] (10 minutes)
   - Purpose: Identify what to act on
   - Prompt: "What patterns do you see? Is the same thing showing up as Green for some and Red for others? Let's discuss the Reds that got multiple votes and the Greens with the most energy."
   - Facilitation tip: Conflicting signals (same item in both Green and Red from different people) are the most valuable discussion points. They reveal disagreements.
6. [Stage: Prioritize] (3 minutes)
   - Purpose: Select top actions
   - Prompt: "Quick vote: Top Red to stop, top Green to start. Go."
   - Facilitation tip: Keep voting fast. 30 seconds, done.
7. [Synthesize] One Green to start, one Red to stop, confirm Yellows. Simple, clean, done.
8. [Close] (3 minutes)
   - Read decisions: "Green light: [X]. Red light: [Y]. Yellow continues: [Z]."
   - Ask: "What color describes how you feel about next sprint?" (Quick pulse)
   - Thank the team. Quick and clean.
</Workflow - Traffic Light Retrospective>
</Instructions>

<Resources>
- ALWAYS add text labels for accessibility. This is non-negotiable
- The visual nature is the value. Use actual colored cards/stickies/circles, not just text
- For conflicting signals (same item in Red AND Green), these are your highest-value discussion points
- Works brilliantly for cross-cultural teams. Traffic lights are universal
- Great as a "first retro ever" format because literally zero explanation is needed
- For remote teams, use colored emoji reactions or colored virtual sticky notes
- The team pulse at the end (what color are you?) creates a trackable morale metric
- Consider running this at the end of any important meeting as a quick feedback mechanism
</Resources>
