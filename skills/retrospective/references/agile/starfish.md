# Starfish Retrospective

<Metadata>
Name: Starfish Retrospective
Category: agile
Aliases: Sea Star, Five-Arm Retrospective, Patrick Kua's Starfish
Duration: 45-60 minutes
Team Size: 4-8 (max 12)
Best For: Teams needing fine-tuning and calibration rather than revolutionary changes
Definition: A five-category retrospective format (Keep Doing, More Of, Less Of, Stop Doing, Start Doing) that provides more nuanced granularity than Start-Stop-Continue by capturing the important difference between amplifying something good versus introducing something entirely new.
</Metadata>

<Gotchas>
1. Use when: Team is generally performing well but needs fine-tuning; Start-Stop-Continue feels too coarse (binary); most practices are "sort of working" but need adjustment; teams that appreciate nuance and gradation; mature teams that have outgrown simpler formats
2. Do NOT use when: New teams (too many categories overwhelm beginners); major revolutionary changes are needed (More/Less nuance dilutes urgency); very short retrospectives (< 30 minutes); team can't distinguish between the five categories
3. Everything in Keep Doing: Team isn't being honest about needed changes. Fix: Challenge gently. Ask "Is everything really perfect? What's one thing that could be 10% better?"
4. Confusing More/Start and Less/Stop: If items are misplaced, the nuance is lost. Fix: Re-clarify distinctions mid-session. Ask "Is this brand new, or amplifying something existing?"
5. No measurement: "More Of pair programming" means nothing without defining current and target state. Fix: Make More/Less measurable, such as "move from 60% to 90% coverage."
6. Ignoring More Of: Teams gravitate toward Stop/Start (dramatic) over More/Less (calibration). Fix: Explicitly ask about each arm and vote across ALL arms.
7. Decision fatigue: Five categories can overwhelm. Fix: Limit to 2-3 items per arm maximum.
</Gotchas>

<Instructions>
<Workflow - Starfish Retrospective
  description="Facilitation guide for Starfish retrospective."
>
1. [Setup] Draw a five-pointed star (or five segments like a pie chart) on the board. Label each arm: "Keep Doing ✅," "More Of ⬆️," "Less Of ⬇️," "Stop Doing 🛑," "Start Doing 🆕." Provide sticky notes. Allocate time: 7 min brainstorm, 10 min placement, 15 min discussion, 8 min actions, 5 min close.
2. [Open] (5 minutes)
   - Read the Prime Directive
   - Frame: "Today we have five options, not just three. We can KEEP things as-is, do MORE of something good, do LESS of something problematic, STOP something entirely, or START something brand new."
   - Clarify distinctions: "More Of = amplify something we already do. Start = introduce something entirely new. Less Of = reduce frequency/scope. Stop = eliminate completely."
3. [Stage: Silent Brainstorm] (7 minutes)
   - Purpose: Generate nuanced reflections across all five dimensions
   - Prompt: "For each arm, think: What should we KEEP exactly as-is? What are we doing that's good but we need MORE of it? What's happening too much, where we need LESS? What should we STOP entirely? What completely NEW thing should we START?"
   - Facilitation tip: If people struggle, give examples: "More Of might be 'pair programming' - we do it sometimes but should do it more. Less Of might be 'context switching' - we can't eliminate it but can reduce it."
4. [Stage: Place & Explain] (10 minutes)
   - Purpose: Make all items visible with brief context
   - Prompt: "Place your notes on the appropriate arm. Give a one-sentence explanation as you place each one."
   - Facilitation tip: Watch for misplacements. If someone puts "start doing daily standups" but you already have them, it might belong in "More Of." Gently ask: "Is this brand new, or amplifying something existing?"
5. [Stage: Group & Vote] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Cluster similar items and democratically prioritize
   - Prompt: "Let's group similar items. Then use your 3 dots to vote on the items with the biggest potential impact."
   - Facilitation tip: Vote across ALL arms, not just Start/Stop. Sometimes the highest-value action is "More Of" something.
6. [Stage: Discuss Top Items] (15 minutes)
   - Purpose: Explore how to implement the highest-priority calibrations
   - Prompt: "For each top-voted item: What specifically would this look like? How do we measure whether we've done More or Less?"
   - Facilitation tip: Make "More/Less" measurable. "More code review" becomes "review every PR within 4 hours" or "move from 60% to 90% coverage."
7. [Synthesize] Convert discussed items into calibration decisions with measurable targets. Each arm may produce different types of action items.
8. [Close] (5 minutes)
   - Read back calibration decisions
   - Ask: "Which arm feels most important for next sprint?"
   - Thank the team for nuanced thinking
</Workflow - Starfish Retrospective>
</Instructions>

<Resources>
- The power of Starfish is NUANCE. If the team collapses it into three categories, you've lost the value; gently redirect
- Use this when the team is past major dysfunction and into optimization territory
- For remote teams, Miro/Mural templates with the five-arm star work beautifully
- After the session, present results as a "calibration dashboard" showing current state → target state for each action
- Great follow-up after using Start-Stop-Continue for several sprints: "Same idea, more precision"
- Ask the team which arm they want to start with. It reveals where their attention naturally goes
</Resources>
