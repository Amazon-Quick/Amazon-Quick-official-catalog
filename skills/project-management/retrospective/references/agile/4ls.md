# 4Ls (Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed For)

<Metadata>
Name: 4Ls (Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed For)
Category: agile
Aliases: Four Ls, 4L Retrospective
Duration: 45-60 minutes
Team Size: 4-8 (max 12)
Best For: Teams focused on growth, learning, and identifying capability gaps after challenging sprints
Definition: A thorough reflection framework that explores four dimensions of team experience - what participants liked, what they learned, what was lacking/missing, and what they longed for - encouraging deeper reflection by adding explicit learning and aspirational dimensions.
</Metadata>

<Gotchas>
1. Use when: After a sprint with significant new challenges or technologies; want to emphasize learning and growth; need to identify capability or resource gaps; post-training or post-onboarding reflection; team is building new skills or adopting new tools
2. Do NOT use when: Quick check-in needed (too detailed for 20 minutes); team is in crisis mode needing immediate action; very new teams still establishing basic processes; routine sprint with nothing new to learn from
3. Confusing Lacked and Longed For: Lacked = absent/missing. Longed For = aspirational/desired. Fix: Clarify this upfront with examples. Draw a timeline showing past-gap vs future-desire.
4. Skipping Learned: The most unique quadrant gets rushed. Fix: Give it deliberate attention; at least 5 dedicated minutes of discussion.
5. All Liked, nothing Lacked: Team avoids identifying gaps. Fix: Push gently. Ask "What would have made this sprint even 10% better?"
6. Longed For becomes fantasy: Items like "unlimited budget" aren't actionable. Fix: Guide toward realistic aspirations achievable within 1-2 sprints.
7. No knowledge capture: Learned items that aren't documented are lost. Fix: Commit to a learning log and publish it after the session.
</Gotchas>

<Instructions>
<Workflow - 4Ls
  description="Facilitation guide for 4Ls (Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed For) retrospective."
>
1. [Setup] Prepare a board with four quadrants: "Liked ❤️," "Learned 📚," "Lacked ❌," and "Longed For ✨." Provide markers and sticky notes in 4 colors (one per quadrant). Allocate time: 10 min reflection, 15 min sharing, 15 min discussion, 5 min actions, 5 min close.
2. [Open] (3 minutes)
   - Read the Prime Directive
   - Frame: "Today we're reflecting across four dimensions. Not just what worked and didn't, but what we LEARNED and what we ASPIRE to. This is about growth."
   - Clarify distinction: "Lacked = something that was missing/absent. Longed For = something aspirational you wish existed."
3. [Stage: Individual Reflection] (10 minutes)
   - Purpose: Deep personal reflection across all four dimensions
   - Prompt: "For each quadrant, write 2-3 items. LIKED: What did you enjoy or appreciate? LEARNED: What new knowledge or insight did you gain? LACKED: What was missing, whether resources, skills, support, or information? LONGED FOR: What do you wish you had? What would make future work better?"
   - Facilitation tip: Give the full 10 minutes. The Learned and Longed For quadrants take more thought than typical retro categories.
4. [Stage: Share by Quadrant] (15 minutes)
   - Purpose: Build collective picture one dimension at a time
   - Prompt: "Let's start with Liked. Everyone place your items and briefly describe them. Then we'll move to Learned, Lacked, and Longed For."
   - Facilitation tip: Process one quadrant at a time (not per-person). This creates coherent themes per dimension.
5. [Stage: Cross-Quadrant Patterns] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Find connections between what was learned, what was lacking, and what's longed for
   - Prompt: "Do you see connections? Does something we Lacked explain something we didn't Learn? Does a Longed For item address a Lacked item?"
   - Facilitation tip: Draw connecting lines between related items across quadrants.
6. [Stage: Prioritize & Act] (10 minutes)
   - Purpose: Convert Lacked and Longed For into actionable improvements
   - Prompt: "Which Lacked items can we address? Which Longed For items are achievable next sprint? Vote on the top 2-3."
   - Facilitation tip: Some Longed For items may be longer-term. Note them as backlog items, not immediate actions.
7. [Synthesize] Document Learned items for the team knowledge base. Create action items from Lacked/Longed For. Celebrate Liked items.
8. [Close] (5 minutes)
   - Share the team's learning log publicly (if appropriate)
   - Ask: "What's one thing you want to learn next sprint?"
   - Thank the team for reflective thinking
</Workflow - 4Ls>
</Instructions>

<Resources>
- The "Learned" column often surfaces the most unique, high-value insights; give it at least 5 dedicated minutes of discussion
- Use this format after sprints involving new tech, new team members, or new processes. It shines when learning is central
- Distinguish clearly at the start between Lacked (past-absent) and Longed For (future-desired). Draw a timeline on the board if needed
- For remote teams, try async pre-fill of all four quadrants, then synchronous discussion of patterns
- Connect Longed For items to sprint planning. They become backlog items or improvement stories
</Resources>
