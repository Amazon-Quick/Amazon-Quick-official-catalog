# Analysis Dimensions Reference

The dimension sets each workflow analyzes samples across, plus the Voice Fidelity scoring rubric used in Voice Validation.

## Fast Track: 12 Rapid Dimensions

Analyze pulled samples (up to 25) across these dimensions, then build fidelity rules (10 in-voice + 10 not-in-voice markers):

1. Voice summary: what makes this voice recognizable in one paragraph.
2. Register detection: which distinct contexts show up in the samples.
3. Tone and rhythm: sentence length, cadence, warmth versus terseness.
4. Hard limits: what this person never does (openings, closings, words).
5. Vocabulary signatures: words and phrases they reach for repeatedly.
6. Fingerprint phrases: exact strings that are unmistakably theirs.
7. Anti-voice markers: phrases that would signal "not them."
8. Analytical style: how they reason through a point.
9. Recommendation style: how they make an ask or propose action.
10. Emotional register: how they express urgency, appreciation, frustration.
11. Rewrite transformations: how they would fix a generic draft.
12. Quantitative fingerprint: average sentence length, list habits, punctuation.

## Voice Fidelity Score (1-10): 7 Dimensions

Used in Voice Validation to score a draft against an existing engram:

1. Structural: does the structure match how they organize a message?
2. Lexical: do the word choices match their vocabulary signatures?
3. Cadence: does sentence length and rhythm match?
4. Judgment: does the reasoning match how they decide?
5. Emotional: does the emotional register match?
6. Channel fidelity: does it fit the register and channel?
7. Anti-voice detection: are any anti-voice markers present?

Score bands:

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Send without editing |
| 7-8 | Minor fixes |
| 5-6 | Needs rewrite |
| 3-4 | Start over |
| 1-2 | Wrong register entirely |
