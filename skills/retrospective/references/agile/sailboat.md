# Sailboat Retrospective

<Metadata>
Name: Sailboat Retrospective
Category: agile
Aliases: Speedboat, Wind & Anchors, Sailing Retrospective
Duration: 45-75 minutes
Team Size: 4-9 (max 15)
Best For: Combining backward reflection with forward-looking risk assessment using an engaging visual metaphor
Definition: A visual metaphor-based retrospective where the team imagines themselves on a sailboat heading toward an island (goal), identifying winds (helping forces), anchors (slowing forces), and rocks (risks ahead), uniquely combining past reflection with forward-looking risk assessment.
</Metadata>

<Gotchas>
1. Use when: Team needs a fresh perspective after text-based formats; want to combine reflection with risk identification; mid-project or mid-quarter checkpoint; team alignment on goals is unclear; cross-functional teams that benefit from visual collaboration
2. Do NOT use when: Very short time available (< 30 minutes); team of highly analytical individuals who resist metaphors; specific process problems need detailed root-cause analysis; team has no clear shared goal to orient toward
3. Visual overload: Making the metaphor too complex with too many elements (sun, currents, sea monsters). Fix: Keep it to 4 categories max: island, wind, anchors, rocks.
4. Anchor obsession: Spending all discussion time on problems while ignoring risks ahead. Fix: Timebox each category equally and enforce the Rocks discussion.
5. Vague island: Not clearly defining the goal, making all other categories unmotivated. Fix: Start with the Island. Get explicit agreement on the destination before anything else.
6. No risk discussion: Skipping or rushing the Rocks category. Fix: This forward-looking element is the unique value of the format. Protect its timebox.
7. Static picture: Never revisiting the sailboat to see if anchors were cut. Fix: Use as a living document. Overlay next retro's sailboat on this one to show progress.
</Gotchas>

<Instructions>
<Workflow - Sailboat Retrospective
  description="Facilitation guide for Sailboat retrospective."
>
1. [Setup] Draw the sailboat scene on a whiteboard or digital canvas: a boat in the center, an island on the right (goal), wind arrows behind (helping), an anchor below (dragging), rocks ahead (risks). Prepare sticky notes in 4 colors. Allocate time: 5 min setup/framing, 20 min brainstorm, 20 min discussion, 10 min actions, 5 min close.
2. [Open] (5 minutes)
   - Read the Prime Directive
   - Frame: "Imagine we're all on this sailboat. The island is where we're headed, our goal. Let's figure out what's helping us sail there, what's slowing us down, and what dangers lie ahead."
   - Ask the team to confirm the Island: "What is our shared goal or destination right now?"
3. [Stage: Define the Island] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Align the team on shared goals before reflecting
   - Prompt: "What does our island look like? What does 'arriving' mean for us this quarter/sprint? Write what success means."
   - Facilitation tip: Get explicit agreement on the goal. This grounds all other discussion. Write it large on the island.
4. [Stage: Identify the Wind] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Acknowledge positive forces propelling the team forward
   - Prompt: "What's blowing us toward our island? What practices, people, tools, or conditions are helping us make progress?"
   - Facilitation tip: Start with wind to build positive energy before anchors.
5. [Stage: Identify the Anchors] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Surface impediments and drag forces
   - Prompt: "What's our anchor? What's dragging in the water, slowing us down? What processes, blockers, or conditions are impeding progress?"
   - Facilitation tip: Press for specifics. "Meetings" is too vague; "45-min standup with 12 people" is actionable.
6. [Stage: Identify the Rocks] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Forward-looking risk identification
   - Prompt: "What rocks are hidden beneath the surface ahead? What risks could damage or sink us if we're not careful? What's coming that we're not prepared for?"
   - Facilitation tip: This forward-looking element is unique to this format. Don't skip or rush it.
7. [Stage: Discuss & Prioritize] (15 minutes)
   - Purpose: Make sense of the visual and decide what to act on
   - Prompt: "Looking at our full picture: What's the biggest anchor we can cut? What risk needs immediate attention? What wind can we amplify?"
   - Facilitation tip: Vote with dots on the most impactful items in each category. Discuss top 1-2 per category.
8. [Synthesize] Create action items: "increase wind" (amplify positives), "cut anchors" (remove impediments), "navigate around rocks" (mitigate risks). Assign owners.
9. [Close] (5 minutes)
   - Take a photo/screenshot of the board
   - Read action items and owners
   - Ask: "How far along is our boat right now, what percentage to the island?"
   - Thank the team for the voyage
</Workflow - Sailboat Retrospective>
</Instructions>

<Resources>
- Draw the visual BEFORE the session begins. Walking into a pre-drawn scene immediately engages participants
- Start with the Island to align on goals. Everything else is relative to the destination
- For remote teams, use Miro/Mural with a pre-made sailboat template
- Revisit the same sailboat image at the next retro to see progress (did we cut those anchors?)
- The Rocks category makes this format ideal for PI Planning or mid-quarter checkpoints
- If the team struggles with the metaphor, rename to simpler terms: Goal, Helps, Blocks, Risks
</Resources>
