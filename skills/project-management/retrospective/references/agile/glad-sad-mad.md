# Glad-Sad-Mad

<Metadata>
Name: Glad-Sad-Mad
Category: agile
Aliases: Mad-Sad-Glad, Emotional Weather Check, Feelings Retro
Duration: 45-60 minutes
Team Size: 4-7 (max 10)
Best For: Surfacing emotional undercurrents and interpersonal dynamics that process-focused retros miss
Definition: An emotion-based retrospective format that asks team members to categorize their experiences by emotional response (what made them glad, sad, or mad), focusing on the emotional dimension of teamwork rather than process mechanics.
</Metadata>

<Gotchas>
1. Use when: Team seems disengaged or emotionally flat; after an intense or stressful sprint; process-focused retros aren't surfacing real issues; interpersonal dynamics need attention; after team changes (new members, departures)
2. Do NOT use when: Team lacks psychological safety; active conflicts between specific members (may escalate); team needs structured process improvement outcomes; very new teams who haven't built trust yet
3. Blame session: "Mad" column turns into finger-pointing at specific individuals. Fix: Redirect to systemic causes. Ask "What about our process created this situation?"
4. Glad-only avoidance: Team only discusses positive items to dodge uncomfortable truths. Fix: Gently push. Ask "What's one thing that was frustrating, even a little?"
5. Therapy session: Discussion spirals into venting without moving toward action. Fix: Timebox emotional processing and transition to root-cause discussion.
6. Forced participation: Pushing someone to share feelings they're not ready to disclose. Fix: Always allow passing. Say "You can pass and come back."
7. No follow-through: Acknowledging emotions but never addressing root causes erodes trust. Fix: Convert top emotional clusters into concrete action items with owners.
</Gotchas>

<Instructions>
<Workflow - Glad-Sad-Mad
  description="Facilitation guide for Glad-Sad-Mad retrospective."
>
1. [Setup] Prepare a board with three columns: "Glad 😊," "Sad 😢," and "Mad 😠." Use colors (green, blue, red) to reinforce the emotional categories. Allocate time: 7 min brainstorm, 15 min sharing, 15 min discussion, 8 min actions, 5 min close.
2. [Open] (5 minutes)
   - Read the Prime Directive
   - Frame explicitly: "Today we're focusing on how we FELT during this sprint. Not just what happened, but how it affected us. All emotions are valid. This is a judgment-free space."
   - Establish ground rules: "We speak from 'I' statements. We listen without interrupting. We don't judge anyone's feelings."
3. [Stage: Silent Reflection] (7 minutes)
   - Purpose: Allow private processing of emotions before group sharing
   - Prompt: "Think about the past sprint. What made you GLAD, happy, energized, grateful? What made you SAD, disappointed, let down, deflated? What made you MAD, frustrated, angry, blocked?"
   - Facilitation tip: Play soft background music. Give the full 7 minutes. Emotional reflection takes longer than process reflection.
4. [Stage: Round-Robin Sharing] (15 minutes)
   - Purpose: Give everyone equal voice and normalize emotional expression
   - Prompt: "Let's go around. Share one item from any column. You can pass and come back."
   - Facilitation tip: Go round-robin (not voluntary) to ensure quiet voices are heard. Acknowledge each share with a brief "thank you" and don't immediately problem-solve.
5. [Stage: Theme Identification] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Find patterns across individual emotions
   - Prompt: "Looking at the board, what patterns do you notice? Are multiple people feeling the same way about something?"
   - Facilitation tip: Look for clusters where 3+ people share similar emotions. These are the real signals.
6. [Stage: Root Cause Discussion] (15 minutes)
   - Purpose: Understand what's causing the strongest negative emotions
   - Prompt: "For our top-voted Sad/Mad themes: What's driving this feeling? What would need to change for this emotion to shift?"
   - Facilitation tip: Keep focus on systemic causes, not individual blame. If someone starts pointing fingers, redirect: "What about our process created this situation?"
7. [Synthesize] Group items into themes. Vote on which emotional clusters to address. Convert the top 2-3 into action items that address root causes.
8. [Close] (5 minutes)
   - Acknowledge the emotional labor: "Thank you for being vulnerable today."
   - Read back action items
   - End with appreciation: "Share one thing you're grateful for about this team"
</Workflow - Glad-Sad-Mad>
</Instructions>

<Resources>
- If you sense low psychological safety, offer anonymous submission (digital tool or folded cards)
- Watch body language: crossed arms, silence, or eye-rolling signal someone is holding back
- It's OK to have a retro where the primary output is "we heard each other." Not every session needs action items
- For remote teams, use video-on to read emotional cues, but don't mandate it
- If Mad items are intensely personal, consider a follow-up 1:1 rather than group processing
- Rotate the column you start with. Always starting with Glad can feel performative
</Resources>
