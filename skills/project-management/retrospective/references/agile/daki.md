# DAKI (Drop, Add, Keep, Improve)

<Metadata>
Name: DAKI (Drop, Add, Keep, Improve)
Category: agile
Aliases: Drop/Add/Keep/Improve, DAKI Framework
Duration: 30-45 minutes
Team Size: 4-8 (max 12)
Best For: Teams needing to declutter processes and explicitly distinguish between maintaining and enhancing practices
Definition: A four-quadrant retrospective focused on inventory management of team practices, evaluating what to Drop (remove entirely), Add (introduce new), Keep (maintain as-is), and Improve (enhance existing), with the explicit "Improve" category distinguishing between practices that are perfect as-is versus those that are good but could be better.
</Metadata>

<Gotchas>
1. Use when: Team needs to declutter processes; accumulated too many practices or ceremonies over time; teams feel overwhelmed and need to simplify; variety alternative to Start-Stop-Continue; need to distinguish "good enough" from "needs enhancement"
2. Do NOT use when: Emotional dynamics are the primary issue; deep exploration of root causes is needed; first-time retrospective participants (slightly complex for absolute beginners); team needs forward-looking risk assessment
3. Everything in Keep: No improvement culture. The team isn't being honest about what could be better. Fix: Push with "What's one thing that's good but not GREAT?"
4. Drop without consensus: Removing practices that some team members depend on without discussion causes resentment. Fix: Require explicit agreement before confirming any Drop decision.
5. Improve without target state: "Improve our standups" means nothing. Fix: Define what the improved version looks like specifically, as "[practice] → [better version]."
6. Add overload: Adding too many new things at once creates change fatigue. Fix: Limit to 1-2 new additions per sprint.
7. No sunset discipline: Agreeing to Drop but never actually stopping the practice. Fix: Give Drop items a specific "sunset date" and check compliance.
</Gotchas>

<Instructions>
<Workflow - DAKI
  description="Facilitation guide for DAKI (Drop, Add, Keep, Improve) retrospective."
>
1. [Setup] Prepare a board with four quadrants labeled "Drop ❌," "Add ➕," "Keep ✅," and "Improve ⬆️." Provide sticky notes in 4 colors. Allocate time: 7 min brainstorm, 10 min sharing/grouping, 10 min discussion, 8 min actions, 5 min close.
2. [Open] (3 minutes)
   - Read the Prime Directive
   - Frame: "Think of this as a practice inventory. We're auditing everything we do and deciding: Should we DROP it? ADD something new? KEEP it exactly as-is? Or IMPROVE it, because it's good but could be better?"
   - Clarify: "Keep = perfect, don't touch. Improve = good, but there's a better version of it."
3. [Stage: Silent Brainstorm] (7 minutes)
   - Purpose: Inventory current practices and generate ideas for each quadrant
   - Prompt: "Review our processes, meetings, tools, and habits. DROP: What's not adding value and should be eliminated? ADD: What new practice would help us? KEEP: What's working perfectly, don't change a thing? IMPROVE: What's good but has room to be better, and how?"
   - Facilitation tip: For Improve items, ask people to write BOTH the current state and what "improved" looks like: "[practice] → [better version]"
4. [Stage: Place & Group] (10 minutes)
   - Purpose: Make all items visible and cluster themes
   - Prompt: "Place your items in the appropriate quadrant. As you place each one, give a one-sentence explanation."
   - Facilitation tip: Watch for items in Keep that should be in Improve. Challenge: "Is this TRULY perfect, or is there a 10% better version?"
5. [Stage: Dot-Vote] (3 minutes)
   - Purpose: Prioritize democratically
   - Prompt: "You have 4 dots, one for each quadrant. Vote on the most impactful item in each quadrant."
   - Facilitation tip: Forcing one vote per quadrant ensures all categories get attention, not just Drop.
6. [Stage: Discuss Top Items] (10 minutes)
   - Purpose: Plan implementation of top-voted decisions
   - Prompt: "For the top Drop: Are we all agreed to remove this? For the top Add: What's the smallest way to start? For the top Improve: What's the specific enhancement?"
   - Facilitation tip: Drop decisions are often the most contentious. Someone owns each practice. Handle with care.
7. [Synthesize] Create action items: Drop items get a "sunset date." Add items get a "trial start date." Improve items get a "target state" definition.
8. [Close] (5 minutes)
   - Read the decisions: "We're dropping X, adding Y, keeping Z, and improving W."
   - Ask: "Does anyone have concerns about any of these decisions?"
   - Thank the team for honest inventory management
</Workflow - DAKI>
</Instructions>

<Resources>
- DAKI works like managing a backlog. Teams familiar with product management intuitively get it
- The "Improve" quadrant is the unique value. It acknowledges that something can be GOOD and still get BETTER
- For Drop items, ask: "What would happen if we stopped doing this tomorrow?" Often the answer is "nothing," and that confirms the Drop
- Limit each quadrant to 3-5 items max for actionability
- Frame the whole exercise as "spring cleaning," decluttering to make room for what matters
- For remote teams, a simple 2x2 grid in any collaboration tool works perfectly
- Review last retro's DAKI at the start of the next one: "Did we actually Drop what we said? Did the Improvements stick?"
</Resources>
