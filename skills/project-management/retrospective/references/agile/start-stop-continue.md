# Start-Stop-Continue

<Metadata>
Name: Start-Stop-Continue
Category: agile
Aliases: Start/Stop/Continue, SSC, Action-Oriented Retro
Duration: 30-45 minutes
Team Size: 3-8 (max 12)
Best For: Quick action-oriented reflection for any team, especially those new to retrospectives
Definition: A three-column framework categorizing feedback into new behaviors to adopt (Start), existing behaviors to eliminate (Stop), and current practices to maintain (Continue).
</Metadata>

<Gotchas>
1. Use when: First-time retro participants; short sprints; action-oriented outcomes needed; format anyone can understand in 30 seconds; sprint with clear wins and misses
2. Do NOT use when: 4+ consecutive sprints with same format (fatigue); deep systemic issues needing root-cause exploration; emotional/interpersonal dynamics; complex incidents needing timeline reconstruction; team knows what to do but lacks motivation
3. Stop column dominance: Session devolves into complaints. Fix: Always start discussion with Continue to set positive tone.
4. Vague actions: "Communicate better" is not an action. Fix: Push for specifics. "Post standup updates in [team chat] by 9:30 AM" is an action.
5. No owners: Unassigned items never complete. Fix: Every action gets a single owner (not "the team") before closing.
6. Too many actions: Teams commit to 8-10 and complete none. Fix: Hard cap at 3-5 items.
7. Format fatigue: Running SSC every sprint for months kills engagement. Fix: Rotate formats every 3-4 sprints.
8. Anchoring: First speaker influences everyone. Fix: Silent brainstorming before sharing eliminates this bias.
</Gotchas>

<Instructions>
<Workflow - Start-Stop-Continue
  description="Facilitation guide for Start-Stop-Continue."
>

1. [Setup] (5 minutes)
   - Prepare a board (physical or digital) with three clearly labeled columns: Start, Stop, Continue
   - Distribute sticky notes or open the digital equivalent
   - Set a visible timer
   - Materials needed: sticky notes (3 colors recommended), markers, timer, board/wall space or digital whiteboard

2. [Open] (3 minutes)
   - State the Prime Directive: "Regardless of what we discover, we understand and truly believe that everyone did the best job they could, given what they knew at the time, their skills and abilities, the resources available, and the situation at hand."
   - Frame the scope: "We are reflecting on [sprint/iteration/period]. Think about what happened during this time."
   - Remind participants: "There are no wrong answers. Be specific when possible. One idea per note."

3. [Stage: Silent Brainstorming] (5-7 minutes)
   - Purpose: Give everyone equal voice without anchoring bias from early speakers
   - Prompt: "Write down your ideas for each column. What should we START doing that we are not doing today? What should we STOP doing because it is not helping us? What should we CONTINUE doing because it is working well?"
   - Facilitation tip: Start the timer visibly. If energy drops early, prompt with "Think about tools, processes, communication, meetings, collaboration patterns."

4. [Stage: Share and Cluster] (10-12 minutes)
   - Purpose: Make all perspectives visible and identify themes
   - Prompt: "Let's go around the table. Place your notes on the board one at a time and briefly explain each one in 15 seconds or less."
   - Facilitation tip: As items go up, actively group similar items. Ask "Is this similar to [other note]?" to cluster. Do not debate items during this stage, just collect and organize.

5. [Stage: Dot-Vote] (3-5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Democratically prioritize what matters most to the team
   - Prompt: "Each person gets 3 votes. Place your dots on the items you think are most important to address. You can put all 3 on one item or spread them out."
   - Facilitation tip: Votes go on clustered themes, not individual notes. Remind the group: "Vote on impact, not just agreement."

6. [Stage: Discuss Top Items] (10-15 minutes)
   - Purpose: Convert the highest-voted items into concrete, assignable actions
   - Prompt: "Let's discuss the top 3-5 voted items. For each one: What specifically would we do? Who will own it? When will it be done?"
   - Facilitation tip: Keep it to 3-5 items maximum. More than 5 action items rarely get completed. Push for specificity: "What does 'communicate better' actually look like as a concrete practice?"

7. [Synthesize] (3 minutes)
   - Read back the action items with owners and dates
   - Confirm the Continue items as team strengths to protect
   - Ask: "Did we miss anything critical?"

8. [Close] (2 minutes)
   - Thank participants for their honesty
   - Remind the team where action items will be tracked
   - State when progress will be reviewed (typically next retro or standup)
   - Optional: one-word checkout, asking each person for a single word describing how they feel about the session

</Workflow - Start-Stop-Continue>
</Instructions>

<Resources>
- Start with Continue, not Stop. It sets a constructive tone and reminds the team that things ARE working.
- Use three different colored sticky notes for the three columns. It makes clustering faster and the board more scannable.
- If remote, use a timer bot and breakout rooms for pairs to brainstorm before the full group share.
- For large teams (10+), split into groups of 4-5 for brainstorming, then merge themes together.
- Track action items from the previous retro at the start of each new one. Accountability closes the loop.
- If the team is quiet during brainstorming, try prompts by category: "Think about our meetings... our code reviews... our deployments... our communication..."
</Resources>
