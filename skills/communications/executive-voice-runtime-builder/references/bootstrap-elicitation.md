# Bootstrap Elicitation Reference

Used by the Bootstrap workflow and by the Native Voice Calibration Prompts procedure (see references/authorship-confidence-gate.md) to generate clean H5-C1 material when discoverable samples are sparse or AI-contaminated.

## Prompt Bank (Native Writing Sprint)

Present 5-10 of these one at a time. Ask the user to respond without AI, in their natural voice. Fragments are fine. Score all responses as H5-C1.

1. Explain a tough decision you made recently and why.
2. Push back on a proposal you disagree with.
3. Recognize a colleague for good work.
4. Escalate a problem to someone senior.
5. Give a status update when you are not sure of the outcome.
6. Ask a peer for help.
7. Report that you are blocked and need something.
8. Explain something complex to someone unfamiliar with it.
9. Rewrite a stiff, formal paragraph in your own words.
10. Introduce yourself to a new team.

## Rewrite Bank (Rewrite and Rejection Test)

Present 2-5 generic AI drafts for the user to revise. Capture what they changed, what sounded wrong, words they would never use, and their preferred structure.

- "I hope this email finds you well. I wanted to reach out to touch base regarding the upcoming project deadline and see if there are any updates you can share at your earliest convenience."
- "Thank you so much for your hard work on this initiative. Your contributions have been invaluable and we truly appreciate everything you bring to the team."
- "I am writing to inform you that we have encountered a blocker that may impact our timeline. We are actively working to resolve this and will keep you apprised of any developments."
- "Moving forward, let's leverage our synergies to align on next steps and ensure we are all on the same page regarding deliverables."
- "Just wanted to circle back on my previous message to see if you had a chance to review the materials I sent over last week."

## Voice Preference Interview

Ask these 10 questions to capture preferences the samples cannot show:

1. What do you never want to sound like?
2. What are the tells that make writing sound AI-generated to you?
3. When you write, do you lead with the ask or the context?
4. How do you open a message to someone you work with often?
5. How do you close a message?
6. Bullets, numbers, or prose for lists?
7. How do you push back when you disagree?
8. How do you signal urgency without being alarming?
9. How do you show appreciation without sounding effusive?
10. What tone do people most often misread in your writing?

## Minimum Viable Gate

Bootstrap may proceed to analysis only when all of the following are met: 5 or more prompt responses, 5 or more rewrites, and 10 or more preference answers. If not met, HALT and explain what is still needed. Do not silently produce a thin engram.
