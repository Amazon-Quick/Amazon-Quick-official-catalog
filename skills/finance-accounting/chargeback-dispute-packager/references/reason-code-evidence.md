# Reason-code families and rebutting evidence

Card networks each use their own numeric reason codes, but disputes fall into a
small set of families. Map the notice to a family below, then use its evidence
list to build the checklist and rebuttal. When a code does not fit cleanly, pick
the closest family and note the ambiguity in the package.

## Fraud / Unauthorized
The cardholder claims they did not authorize the transaction.
Evidence that typically rebuts it:
- AVS match (billing address verified) and CVV/CVC match at authorization.
- 3-D Secure or other issuer authentication result for the transaction.
- Proof of delivery to the AVS-verified address, ideally with signature.
- Prior undisputed orders from the same cardholder or account.
- Device, IP, or account login data linking the order to the cardholder.

## Product Not Received
The cardholder claims the goods or services never arrived.
Evidence that typically rebuts it:
- Carrier tracking showing delivery, with carrier name and delivery date.
- Delivery confirmation or signature at the address on the order.
- Match between the delivery address and the order/billing address.
- For digital goods: access, download, or activation logs with timestamps.
- Customer communications acknowledging receipt or arranging delivery.

## Not as Described / Defective
The cardholder claims the item differed from its description or was defective.
Evidence that typically rebuts it:
- The product listing or description the customer purchased from.
- Photos or specifications showing the item matched the description.
- Terms and conditions the customer accepted at checkout.
- Communications resolving or addressing the complaint.
- The refund/return policy and any repair or replacement offered.

## Duplicate / Processing Error
The cardholder claims they were billed twice or the charge was processed in error.
Evidence that typically rebuts it:
- Transaction records showing two distinct purchases (different items/orders).
- Itemized receipts or invoices for each separate charge.
- Authorization and settlement logs showing a single valid charge.
- Proof that any duplicate was already voided or refunded, if applicable.

## Subscription / Recurring / Cancelled
The cardholder disputes a recurring charge or claims they cancelled.
Evidence that typically rebuts it:
- The signed or accepted recurring-billing agreement and its terms.
- The cancellation policy and how/when the customer had to cancel.
- Proof the service was active and used during the billed period.
- The date of any cancellation request versus the billing date.
- Notice sent to the customer before the renewal charge, if any.

## Credit Not Processed / Refund
The cardholder claims a promised refund or credit was never issued.
Evidence that typically rebuts it:
- Proof the refund or credit was already issued, with date and reference.
- The refund/return policy showing no refund was owed, if that is the case.
- Communications about the refund request and its resolution.
