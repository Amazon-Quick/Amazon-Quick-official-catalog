# Feedback Grid (Plus/Delta)

<Metadata>
Name: Feedback Grid (Plus/Delta)
Category: agile
Aliases: Plus Delta, +/Δ, Things Working/Things to Change
Duration: 15-25 minutes
Team Size: 3-10 (max 30+)
Best For: Quick, frequent feedback with constructive framing - scales to large groups and can be used for any meeting, not just retros
Definition: A two-column format using Plus (+) for what's working well and Delta (Δ) for what needs to change, where the linguistic choice of "delta" (change) instead of "minus" (bad) intentionally creates constructive rather than critical framing.
</Metadata>

<Gotchas>
1. Use when: Quick feedback needed (end of training, meeting, or workshop); teams that respond negatively to "what went wrong" framing; very frequent retrospectives (daily or multiple times per week); supplemental format alongside deeper periodic retros; large groups where complex formats become unwieldy; non-technical or cross-functional workshops
2. Do NOT use when: Need multi-dimensional analysis of complex issues; complex organizational issues requiring root-cause investigation; "change" is too vague and need specific action categories; team needs emotional processing (too clinical)
3. Delta as complaint box: "Delta: everything sucks." Fix: Redirect. Ask "What specifically would you CHANGE it to? What's the proposed new state?"
4. No specificity in Deltas: "Change our process" means nothing. Fix: Always push for the proposed new state, as "[current] → [proposed change]."
5. Skipping the Plus column: Jumping to problems without acknowledging strengths. Fix: Require equal contribution to both columns.
6. Overthinking the format: This is meant to be FAST. Fix: If you need 60 minutes, use a different format. Keep this under 25 minutes.
7. Confusing with RAG status: Plus/Delta is about practices to continue/change, not project health indicators. Fix: Clarify the distinction upfront if the team uses RAG elsewhere.
</Gotchas>

<Instructions>
<Workflow - Plus/Delta
  description="Facilitation guide for Plus/Delta (Feedback Grid) retrospective."
>
1. [Setup] Prepare a board with two columns: "+ (Plus)" and "Δ (Delta)." Use green and blue sticky notes. This format is intentionally minimal. Allocate time: 3 min brainstorm, 5 min share, 7 min discuss, 3 min close.
2. [Open] (2 minutes)
   - Frame: "Two questions: What's working, our Pluses? And what needs to change, our Deltas? Note: Delta means CHANGE, not BAD. We're identifying what to evolve, not what to criticize."
   - Emphasize: "Delta is not 'minus.' It's the Greek letter for change. We're asking: what should be DIFFERENT?"
3. [Stage: Silent Brainstorm] (3 minutes)
   - Purpose: Quick individual reflection with constructive framing
   - Prompt: "Write your Pluses: What IS working? What should we keep? And your Deltas: What needs to CHANGE? What would you alter, adjust, or evolve?"
   - Facilitation tip: The short time is intentional. This format rewards gut reactions, not overthinking.
4. [Stage: Share] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Surface all feedback quickly
   - Prompt: "Place your notes. Quick one-liner for each."
   - Facilitation tip: For large groups (15+), have people silently place notes without verbal explanation. Read them after.
5. [Stage: Quick Discussion] (7 minutes)
   - Purpose: Convert Deltas into specific change proposals
   - Prompt: "For our top Delta items: What SPECIFICALLY would the changed version look like? Not just 'change this' but 'change it TO what?'"
   - Facilitation tip: Press for specificity. "Change our standups" becomes "Change standups from 30 min to 15 min with async updates for blockers."
6. [Synthesize] Convert top 1-2 Deltas into specific change proposals with owners. Affirm Pluses.
7. [Close] (2 minutes)
   - Read change proposals
   - Quick: "Anything else? No? Great. Let's go."
   - Keep it crisp. This format respects people's time.
</Workflow - Plus/Delta>
</Instructions>

<Resources>
- The linguistic choice of "Delta" over "Minus" is the ENTIRE value proposition. Emphasize it
- This format scales to 30+ people because it's so simple. Great for all-hands feedback
- Can be used at the end of ANY meeting, not just retrospectives: "Quick Plus/Delta on this meeting?"
- For daily standups that feel stale, add a 2-minute Plus/Delta at the end weekly
- Combine with other formats: use Plus/Delta as a quick weekly check-in + a deeper monthly retro
- For remote teams, drop a Plus/Delta poll in [team chat] every Friday: "One plus, one delta for the week"
- The format is culturally neutral and works across languages. The symbols are universal
</Resources>
