# What Went Well / What Didn't

<Metadata>
Name: What Went Well / What Didn't
Category: agile
Aliases: WWW/WD, Went Well / Didn't Go Well, Plus/Minus, Worked/Didn't Work
Duration: 15-30 minutes
Team Size: 3-7 (max 15)
Best For: Quick pulse checks when time is limited or the team is new to retrospectives
Definition: The simplest possible retrospective format with two columns asking what went well and what didn't, offering zero learning curve, zero explanation needed, and immediate value as a starting point before teams graduate to more sophisticated formats.
</Metadata>

<Gotchas>
1. Use when: Very limited time (15-20 minutes); first-ever retrospective for a team; simplicity is valued over sophistication; quick pulse check between more thorough retrospectives; teams skeptical of "fancy" formats
2. Do NOT use when: Team has been doing this for months (stale and unproductive); need nuanced, multi-dimensional reflection; complex problems requiring structured analysis; team needs forward-looking elements (risks, experiments)
3. Only "Went Well" items: Team avoids conflict by only listing positives. Fix: Push. Ask "Surely something was frustrating? Even one small thing."
4. Making "Didn't Go Well" personal: "John missed the deadline." Fix: Redirect to systemic. Say "Our handoff process failed."
5. No action items: Just listing things without converting to actions is venting, not improving. Fix: Always select top 1-2 items and assign owners.
6. Using this every sprint forever: After 4-5 uses, teams need more sophisticated formats or this becomes rote. Fix: Graduate to Start-Stop-Continue or other formats after building confidence.
7. Long discussions: This format is for SPEED. Fix: If you need 60 minutes of discussion, use a different format. Enforce tight timeboxes.
</Gotchas>

<Instructions>
<Workflow - What Went Well / What Didn't
  description="Facilitation guide for What Went Well / What Didn't retrospective."
>
1. [Setup] Prepare a board with two columns: "What Went Well ✅" and "What Didn't Go Well ❌." Optional third column: "What to Try Next 💡." Provide sticky notes. Allocate time: 5 min brainstorm, 5 min share, 10 min discuss, 5 min actions.
2. [Open] (2 minutes)
   - Read the Prime Directive (abbreviated if time is very tight)
   - Frame: "Simple question today: What went well, and what didn't? Let's be honest and brief."
   - No complex setup needed. The format explains itself.
3. [Stage: Silent Brainstorm] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Quick individual reflection
   - Prompt: "Write down what WENT WELL this sprint, including wins, successes, things you're glad about. Then write what DIDN'T GO WELL, including frustrations, failures, things that went wrong. One item per note."
   - Facilitation tip: Even with only 5 minutes, insist on silence. Groupthink is the enemy of honest reflection.
4. [Stage: Share] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Make all items visible quickly
   - Prompt: "Place your notes. Give a one-liner for each. No long explanations needed."
   - Facilitation tip: Go fast. This format's strength is speed. Don't let sharing become a storytelling session.
5. [Stage: Quick Discussion] (10 minutes)
   - Purpose: Surface patterns and decide what to do
   - Prompt: "What patterns do you see? For the 'Didn't Go Well' items: What's one thing we could do differently next time?"
   - Facilitation tip: Don't discuss every item. Identify the top 1-2 themes and focus there. Ask: "If we could only fix one thing, what would it be?"
6. [Synthesize] Convert the top 1-2 "Didn't Go Well" items into specific action items. Acknowledge "Went Well" items.
7. [Close] (2 minutes)
   - Read 1-3 action items
   - Quick round: "One word. How do you feel about next sprint?"
   - Done. Keep it crisp.
</Workflow - What Went Well / What Didn't>
</Instructions>

<Resources>
- This is your emergency format. When you have 15 minutes and need to do SOMETHING, use this
- Perfect as a "between retros" pulse check in addition to a monthly deep-dive format
- For very new teams, start here and graduate to Start-Stop-Continue after 2-3 uses
- The optional "What to Try Next" column transforms this from pure reflection into action. Add it when the team is ready
- For remote teams, a simple shared doc with two columns works. No fancy tools needed
- Consider using this format asynchronously: post two columns in [team chat], collect responses over 24 hours, then summarize
</Resources>
