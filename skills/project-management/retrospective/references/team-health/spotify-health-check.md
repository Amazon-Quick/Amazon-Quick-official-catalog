# Spotify Health Check

Multi-dimensional team health assessment using traffic-light ratings across key dimensions, tracked over time to surface patterns and guide improvement focus.

<Metadata>
Name: Spotify Health Check
Category: team-health
Aliases: Squad Health Check, Team Health Assessment, Team Health Check (Spotify Model)
Duration: 30–60 minutes
Team Size: 4–12
Best For: Multi-dimensional team health visualization with traffic-light ratings tracked over time to surface patterns
Definition: A structured assessment where teams rate themselves across multiple dimensions of team effectiveness using a traffic light system (Green/Yellow/Red) and trend arrows (↑/→/↓).
</Metadata>

<Gotchas>
1. Use when: Monthly or quarterly cadence for ongoing health monitoring; When you want to track trends over time (single snapshots are less valuable); When managing multiple teams and need comparable data across squads; When team issues are vague and need structured surfacing; When team needs a diagnostic before deciding which retro format to use next
2. Do NOT use when: Weekly cadence (too frequent and heavy for this format); When immediate action items are needed (this is diagnostic, not prescriptive); If management will use results punitively (destroys honesty); When the team needs deep discussion rather than broad assessment
3. Using health data for performance evaluation: If ratings are used in performance reviews or team comparisons that affect rewards, honesty evaporates instantly. Fix: Health check data is for the TEAM's use; never for external evaluation.
4. Never following up on Red items: Rating dimensions Red repeatedly without actually taking action erodes trust in the process. Fix: Focus areas MUST result in concrete action. If you can't address it, explain why honestly.
5. All Green team: Every dimension rated Green every time; either the team is perfect (unlikely) or people aren't being honest. Fix: Normalize Yellow/Red as healthy data. Leaders should model honesty: "I think our Tech Quality is actually Yellow because..."
6. Dimension overload: Assessing 15+ dimensions makes the session exhausting and ratings superficial. Fix: Start with Spotify's 11 and customize. Drop dimensions that are consistently Green and add context-specific ones.
7. One-time snapshot: Running a health check once without establishing cadence. Fix: The value is in trends over time. A single assessment is baseline; ongoing tracking reveals patterns.
</Gotchas>

<Instructions>
<Workflow - Spotify Health Check
  description="Step-by-step facilitation guide for Spotify Health Check."
>
1. [Setup] (Day before)
   - Select dimensions to assess (start with Spotify's 11, customize over time)
   - Prepare cards or digital board with each dimension and its description
   - For each dimension, prepare two anchors: "Awesome" description and "Crappy" description
   - Have previous assessment results ready for trend comparison
   - Prepare voting mechanism (colored cards, digital polls, or anonymous survey)

2. [Open] (5 minutes)
   - State purpose: "We're taking our team's vital signs across multiple dimensions - not to judge, but to identify where to focus our improvement energy"
   - Explain rating system: 🟢 Green (awesome, no problems), 🟡 Yellow (some concerns, not terrible), 🔴 Red (significant problems, needs attention)
   - Explain trends: ↑ Improving, → Stable, ↓ Getting worse
   - Remind: "This is about honest assessment, not about looking good. Red is valuable data, not failure."

3. [Stage: Individual Assessment] (15 minutes)
   - Purpose: Get each person's honest rating across all dimensions without group influence
   - Prompt: "For each dimension, rate where our team is right now: Green, Yellow, or Red. Then indicate the trend: improving, stable, or declining."
   - Facilitation tip: Do this simultaneously and silently. If using physical cards, have each person show their card at the same time (avoid anchoring). If digital, use anonymous voting. Show descriptions of "awesome" and "crappy" for each dimension so ratings are calibrated.

4. [Stage: Reveal & Discuss Disagreements] (20 minutes)
   - Purpose: Surface areas where the team has different perceptions (high variance = misalignment)
   - Prompt: "Let's look at where we disagree. For dimensions where ratings span Green to Red - what's causing different experiences on the same team?"
   - Facilitation tip: Don't discuss every dimension - focus on (a) dimensions with high variance (disagreement), and (b) dimensions rated Red. Variance often reveals that different roles/individuals experience the team differently. This insight alone is valuable.

5. [Stage: Trend Analysis] (10 minutes)
   - Purpose: Identify what's improving vs. declining over time
   - Prompt: "Looking at our trends compared to last assessment - what's getting better? What's getting worse? What's stuck?"
   - Facilitation tip: This only works if you have historical data. Show the trajectory. A Yellow with an ↑ trend is healthier than a Green with a ↓ trend. Celebrate improvements. Flag declining dimensions for immediate attention.

6. [Stage: Focus Selection] (10 minutes)
   - Purpose: Choose 1–2 dimensions to actively improve before next assessment
   - Prompt: "We can't fix everything at once. Which 1–2 dimensions will we commit to improving before our next health check?"
   - Facilitation tip: Don't pick more than 2 focus areas; teams that try to improve everything improve nothing. Selection criteria: Red + declining trend (urgent), or dimension that would unblock other dimensions (high-impact point). Vote if consensus doesn't emerge.

7. [Synthesize] (5 minutes)
   - Record all ratings and trends
   - Confirm 1–2 focus areas and initial ideas for improvement
   - Schedule the next health check (maintain consistent cadence)

8. [Close] (5 minutes)
   - Thank the team for honest assessment
   - Confirm: "Our focus areas for the next [month/quarter] are [X] and [Y]"
   - Quick check-out: "One thing you appreciate about this team right now"
</Workflow - Spotify Health Check>
</Instructions>

<Resources>
- Customize the 11 Spotify dimensions to your context. They're starting points, not gospel. A data science team might replace "Easy to Release" with "Data Quality." A marketing team might add "Creative Freedom."
- High variance on a single dimension is often MORE interesting than a Red rating. It means different people on the same team are having fundamentally different experiences. This warrants deep discussion.
- Track results over time using a simple spreadsheet or radar chart. The visual trajectory is powerful; teams can see improvement (or decline) that's invisible in daily work.
- For organizations with multiple teams, consider aggregating health check data to identify systemic issues vs. team-specific ones. If 6 of 8 teams rate "Support" Red, that's an organizational issue, not a team issue.
- The "Pawns or Players" dimension (autonomy) is often the most revealing and the hardest for managers to hear. Teams rated Red on autonomy are typically also Red on Fun and Mission. Address autonomy first as a high-impact point.
- Consider using anonymous digital voting rather than visible card-holding, especially if there's any power dynamic concern. The data is more honest when voting is private.
</Resources>
