# Common Coverage Blind Spots by Line of Business

Lookup data for surfacing considerations to discuss with a licensed agent. These are common, well-known coverage limitations, not universal truths: always defer to the specific policy language the user provided. Frame every item neutrally as something to discuss, never as a directive to buy.

To extend this reference, add a new line-of-business section using the same heading and bullet format.

## Home / Homeowners
- Flood damage is typically excluded and requires a separate flood policy.
- Earthquake and earth-movement damage is typically excluded.
- Sewer or drain backup is often excluded or requires an endorsement.
- Actual cash value (ACV) vs. replacement cost: ACV settlements deduct depreciation and may leave a rebuild gap.
- High-value items (jewelry, art, collectibles) are often capped by sub-limits and may need scheduling.
- Ordinance-or-law coverage (bringing a rebuild up to current code) is often limited.
- Home business or short-term rental activity is often excluded.

## Renters
- The policy covers personal property and liability, not the building structure.
- Flood and earthquake are typically excluded.
- High-value items may be subject to sub-limits.
- Roommates not named on the policy are usually not covered.

## Auto
- Gap between loan/lease balance and vehicle actual cash value is not covered without gap coverage.
- Rideshare or delivery use is typically excluded under a personal auto policy.
- Business use of the vehicle may be excluded.
- Rental reimbursement and roadside assistance are often optional add-ons.
- Uninsured/underinsured motorist limits may be lower than liability limits.

## Life
- Coverage amount may not reflect current dependents, income replacement needs, or debts.
- Term policies expire; coverage may lapse before dependents are independent.
- Exclusions may apply for the first two years (contestability) or for specific causes.

## Health
- Out-of-network care, high deductibles, and out-of-pocket maximums drive exposure.
- Specific ongoing or specialty treatments may be excluded or limited.
- Prescription tiers and formularies can leave notable cost gaps.

## Umbrella / Excess Liability
- Underlying policy limits must usually meet a threshold before umbrella coverage applies.
- Certain liabilities (business, professional, intentional acts) are often excluded.
