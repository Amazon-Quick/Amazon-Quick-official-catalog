# GHG Protocol Scopes and Categories

The GHG Protocol Corporate Standard organizes emissions into three scopes. Use this
file to classify each emission source before applying a factor.

## Scope 1: Direct Emissions

Emissions from sources the organization owns or controls.

- **1A Stationary combustion**: boilers, furnaces, generators, heaters.
- **1B Mobile combustion**: owned or controlled vehicles (fleet cars, trucks, forklifts).
- **1C Fugitive emissions**: refrigerant leaks, SF6 from switchgear, CH4 from gas systems.
- **1D Process emissions**: chemical reactions in manufacturing, not combustion related.

## Scope 2: Indirect Energy Emissions

Emissions from purchased energy. The GHG Protocol Scope 2 Guidance (2015) requires
**both** a location-based and a market-based result.

- **2A** Purchased electricity
- **2B** Purchased steam
- **2C** Purchased heating
- **2D** Purchased cooling

## Scope 3: Value Chain Emissions (15 categories)

Assess all 15 for relevance. Calculate those with available data. Document which
categories are excluded and why.

| Cat | Category | Typical method |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Purchased goods and services | spend-based or weight-based |
| 2 | Capital goods | spend-based |
| 3 | Fuel and energy activities not in Scope 1 or 2 | activity-based |
| 4 | Upstream transportation and distribution | distance or spend |
| 5 | Waste generated in operations | weight by disposal method |
| 6 | Business travel (air, rail, rental car, hotel) | distance or nights |
| 7 | Employee commuting | distance by mode |
| 8 | Upstream leased assets | activity-based |
| 9 | Downstream transportation and distribution | distance or spend |
| 10 | Processing of sold products | activity-based |
| 11 | Use of sold products | activity-based |
| 12 | End-of-life treatment of sold products | weight by disposal method |
| 13 | Downstream leased assets | activity-based |
| 14 | Franchises | activity-based |
| 15 | Investments | equity-share or financed-emissions |

## Data quality notes

- Scope 3 Category 1 (purchased goods) often dominates the total but relies on
  spend-based factors (lower accuracy) or supplier-specific data (hard to obtain).
  Flag the uncertainty level whenever it is used.
- Biogenic CO2 (from biomass combustion) is calculated but reported separately from
  fossil emissions. Do not include it in scope totals.
