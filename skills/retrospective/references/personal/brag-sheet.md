# Brag Sheet

<Metadata>
Name: Brag Sheet
Category: personal
Aliases: Brag Doc, Accomplishment Log, Impact Tracker, Win Sheet, Self-Assessment Prep
Duration: 30–60 minutes monthly; 2–4 hours for annual compilation
Team Size: 1 (individual practice)
Best For: Cataloguing accomplishments and impact in Action → Result → Evidence format for performance reviews, promotions, and self-advocacy
Definition: A structured retrospective focused specifically on cataloguing accomplishments, impact, and evidence for performance reviews, promotions, or self-advocacy.
</Metadata>

<Gotchas>
1. Use when: Monthly updates throughout the year (ideal cadence); Before performance reviews (compile and format existing entries); When preparing promotion packets or career advancement cases; Before job interviews (evidence-based confidence building); When experiencing imposter syndrome (concrete evidence counters self-doubt); When your work is invisible or underrecognized
2. Do NOT use when: Team retrospectives (this is strictly individual); When team accomplishments are more relevant than individual contributions; As a substitute for genuine performance improvement (brag sheets don't fix actual gaps)
3. Review-time only: Only updating the brag sheet the week before performance reviews, relying on 12 months of hazy memory. Fix: Monthly updates take 30 minutes. Annual reconstruction takes 4+ hours and misses most contributions. The monthly habit is non-negotiable.
4. Activity listing without impact: Recording what you DID without what RESULTED. "Attended meetings" and "wrote code" aren't accomplishments. Fix: Every entry must have a result. If you can't state the impact, either find it or question whether the activity was valuable.
5. Modesty filter: Downplaying contributions out of politeness or imposter syndrome. Fix: This document is factual evidence, not bragging. Record accurately. You're not claiming to be amazing; you're documenting what happened.
6. Solo contributions only: Only capturing individual output while ignoring leadership, influence, and enabling others' success. Fix: "Multiplier" contributions (mentoring, unblocking, aligning, influencing) are often the most promotion-relevant. Capture them explicitly.
7. No evidence links: Claiming impact without linking to proof. Fix: Save screenshots of praise, bookmark dashboards showing metrics, save relevant Slack threads. Evidence turns claims into facts.
</Gotchas>

<Instructions>
<Workflow - Brag Sheet
  description="Step-by-step facilitation guide for Brag Sheet."
>
1. [Setup] (One-time, then recurring)
   - Create your brag document (choose format: doc, spreadsheet, or structured notes)
   - Set a recurring monthly calendar reminder: "Update brag sheet"
   - Bookmark sources to mine: Slack search (your messages), email sent folder, calendar (meetings you led), code repositories, project documents
   - If available, review your level/role expectations or promotion criteria to understand what "impact" means at your level

2. [Open] (3 minutes)
   - Open your brag document and review the last few entries to reconnect with the format
   - Set intention: "I'm here to honestly capture what I accomplished and the impact it created - not to be modest, not to inflate, but to accurately represent my contributions"
   - Open your evidence sources: calendar, Slack, email, project tools

3. [Stage: Mine Sources] (15 minutes)
   - Purpose: Gather raw material from the past month by mining your digital trail
   - Prompt: "What did I ship, deliver, or complete? What did I lead or influence? What problems did I solve? What did I help OTHERS accomplish?"
   - Facilitation tip: Walk through your calendar week by week, what meetings did you lead? What presentations did you give? Walk through Slack, what questions did you answer? What decisions did you drive? Walk through your task completions, what shipped? Don't filter yet, capture everything.

4. [Stage: Structure as Impact] (15 minutes)
   - Purpose: Convert raw accomplishments into Action → Result → Evidence format
   - Prompt: "For each accomplishment: What did I DO (action)? What RESULTED from it (outcome)? What EVIDENCE proves it (metrics, quotes, links)?"
   - Facilitation tip: Transform activities into impact statements. BAD: "Attended 15 design reviews." GOOD: "Led design review process for Project X, catching 3 architectural issues pre-implementation that would have required 2-week rework, saving estimated 120 engineering hours." Quantify everything possible: time saved, money saved, users impacted, quality improved.

5. [Stage: Capture Influence & Leadership] (10 minutes)
   - Purpose: Document contributions that extend beyond individual output - mentoring, leading, influencing direction
   - Prompt: "Who did I help succeed? Where did I influence a decision or direction? What did I mentor, teach, or unblock for others? Where did I lead without formal authority?"
   - Facilitation tip: This is where senior-level impact lives and where most people under-document. Include: mentoring conversations, cross-team alignment you drove, decisions you influenced, culture contributions, hiring/interviewing. These "multiplier" contributions are often promotion-critical.

6. [Stage: Note Skills Developed] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Track capability growth alongside output
   - Prompt: "What new skills or capabilities did I build this month? What stretched me beyond my comfort zone?"
   - Facilitation tip: Include both technical skills (learned new framework, mastered new tool) and professional skills (improved presentation ability, navigated conflict, influenced without authority). Growth evidence is valuable for promotion narratives.

7. [Synthesize] (5 minutes)
   - Review new entries for completeness (Action + Result + Evidence)
   - Tag entries by category (delivery, leadership, technical excellence, collaboration)
   - Note any evidence to collect later (screenshot a Slack praise, bookmark a metric)
   - File the update

8. [Close] (2 minutes)
   - Appreciate yourself: read through this month's entries and acknowledge the work
   - Set reminder for next month
   - If preparing for a review: note which entries are strongest/most aligned with expectations
</Workflow - Brag Sheet>
</Instructions>

<Resources>
- Set a recurring monthly calendar reminder. The #1 reason brag sheets fail is irregular updating. Make it a non-negotiable 30-minute monthly practice.
- Mine Slack with search operators: your messages, channels you're active in, reactions your messages received. Slack archives contain evidence of contributions you've forgotten.
- Include things you helped OTHERS accomplish. Senior-level impact is about multiplying; if you unblocked a colleague, enabled a team's success, or mentored someone's growth, that's YOUR impact too.
- Quantify aggressively. "Saved time" → "Saved an estimated 40 engineering hours per sprint." "Improved quality" → "Reduced P1 bugs from 5/sprint to 1/sprint." Numbers transform claims into evidence.
- Align entries with your promotion criteria or level expectations. If "cross-team influence" is a requirement for the next level, ensure you're capturing (and seeking) those opportunities.
- This document is also useful for job interviews. Impact statements in Action → Result → Evidence format map directly to behavioral interview questions (STAR format).
</Resources>
