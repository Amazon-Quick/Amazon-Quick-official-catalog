# Hot Air Balloon

<Metadata>
Name: Hot Air Balloon
Category: agile
Aliases: Balloon Retrospective, Rise & Fall Retro
Duration: 45-60 minutes
Team Size: 4-8 (max 12)
Best For: Combining backward reflection with aspirational forward-looking elements when teams need both analysis and inspiration
Definition: A visual metaphor retrospective using a hot air balloon where flames represent forces pushing upward (positive), sandbags represent forces pulling down (obstacles), storm clouds represent future risks, and sunny skies represent the team's vision/aspirations, uniquely combining retrospective with futurespective elements.
</Metadata>

<Gotchas>
1. Use when: Team needs to look forward as well as backward; mid-project checkpoints or start of quarter; after a particularly challenging period (need aspirational element); risk identification is important alongside reflection; teams at inflection points needing both grounding and vision
2. Do NOT use when: Need deep root-cause analysis of specific issues; team needs pure backward-looking reflection; extremely short time window (< 40 minutes); team needs concrete process fixes, not inspiration
3. Ignoring Sunny Skies: Skipping the aspirational element reduces this to just another problem-identification format. Fix: Protect this stage; it's what differentiates this format from Sailboat or Anchors & Engines.
4. All sandbags, no flames: Team only sees problems. Fix: Push for balance: "What IS working? What's giving us any lift at all?"
5. Vague storms: "Things might go wrong" isn't helpful. Fix: Demand specificity: "the Q3 launch with no QA plan" not "the deadline."
6. No connection to vision: Action items that don't align with the sunny skies destination are directionless. Fix: Test each action against the vision: "Does this move us toward our sunny skies?"
7. Over-decorated visual: Adding too many metaphor elements (birds, other balloons) confuses the framework. Fix: Stick to four elements; flames, sandbags, storms, sunny skies.
</Gotchas>

<Instructions>
<Workflow - Hot Air Balloon
  description="Facilitation guide for Hot Air Balloon retrospective."
>
1. [Setup] Draw or display the hot air balloon visual: balloon at center, flames below it, sandbags hanging from basket, storm clouds ahead, sunny skies above. Use colored sticky notes: orange/red (flames), gray (sandbags), dark blue (storms), yellow (sunny skies). Allocate time: 5 min framing, 20 min brainstorm (5 per category), 15 min discussion, 10 min actions, 5 min close.
2. [Open] (5 minutes)
   - Read the Prime Directive
   - Frame: "We're in a hot air balloon together. Some things are making us rise, giving us lift. Some things are weighing us down. There are storms ahead we should prepare for, and sunny skies we're aiming for. Let's map all of it."
   - Point to each visual element as you explain it
3. [Stage: Hot Air / Flames] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Identify what's giving the team lift and momentum
   - Prompt: "What's our hot air? What's giving us lift right now? What practices, wins, or conditions are propelling us upward?"
   - Facilitation tip: Start here to build energy. These are your fuel. The team should feel buoyed.
4. [Stage: Sandbags] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Identify what's weighing the team down
   - Prompt: "What sandbags are we carrying? What's weighing us down, making it harder to rise? What would we drop if we could?"
   - Facilitation tip: The "drop" framing is powerful. It implies these are things we're CHOOSING to carry. Ask: "Can we cut this loose?"
5. [Stage: Storm Clouds] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Forward-looking risk and threat identification
   - Prompt: "What storms do you see on the horizon? What problems, risks, or challenges are heading our way that we're not yet prepared for?"
   - Facilitation tip: Push for specifics: "the deadline" is vague; "the Q3 launch with no QA plan" is actionable.
6. [Stage: Sunny Skies] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Define the aspirational vision. Where does the team want to go?
   - Prompt: "What does sunny skies look like for us? What's our ideal destination? If everything goes well, where do we land?"
   - Facilitation tip: This aspirational element is what makes this format unique. Don't skip it even if time is tight.
7. [Stage: Prioritize & Act] (15 minutes)
   - Purpose: Convert the visual into actionable decisions
   - Prompt: "Looking at our full picture: What's the heaviest sandbag we can cut? What storm do we need to prepare for now? How do we generate more hot air?"
   - Facilitation tip: Dot-vote on sandbags and storms. Discuss top 2-3 items.
8. [Synthesize] Create three types of actions: amplify hot air, cut sandbags, prepare for storms. Align all actions toward sunny skies vision.
9. [Close] (5 minutes)
   - Take a photo/screenshot of the balloon visual
   - Read action items
   - Ask: "How high are we flying right now, on a scale of 1-10?"
   - End with: "Let's keep rising."
</Workflow - Hot Air Balloon>
</Instructions>

<Resources>
- The four-element structure creates natural energy flow: positive → problems → risks → aspiration
- Limit to 5 minutes per category STRICTLY to maintain momentum. Set visible timers
- For remote teams, use a collaborative canvas (Miro/Mural) with a pre-drawn balloon template
- This format excels at mid-project checkpoints where you need both "how'd we get here" and "where are we going"
- The "altitude check" (1-10 scale) at the end creates a trackable metric you can compare across retros
- If running this quarterly, overlay the new balloon on last quarter's to show progress
</Resources>
