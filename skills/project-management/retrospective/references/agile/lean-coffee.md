# Lean Coffee Retro

<Metadata>
Name: Lean Coffee Retro
Category: agile
Aliases: Lean Coffee, Democratized Agenda Retro, Participant-Led Retro
Duration: 30-60 minutes
Team Size: 4-10 (max 15)
Best For: Teams that feel retrospectives don't address their real concerns - fully democratic, participant-driven agenda
Definition: A participant-driven retrospective format where the team democratically generates and prioritizes the discussion agenda using a Kanban board (To Discuss → Discussing → Discussed), with strict time-boxing and thumb-votes to continue or move on.
</Metadata>

<Gotchas>
1. Use when: Team feels retrospectives don't address their real concerns; want maximum democratic participation; engagement is low in facilitator-structured formats; cross-functional teams with diverse unpredictable concerns; don't know what topics the team needs to discuss
2. Do NOT use when: New teams that need more structure and guidance; a specific topic MUST be discussed (format lets the team bypass it); teams with dominant personalities who will control voting; facilitator has critical information to present
3. One topic dominates: Failing to enforce timeboxes lets a single passionate topic consume the entire session. Fix: Be ruthless with the timer. Maximum one 3-minute extension per topic, then move on.
4. Loudest voice wins: Without strict voting and turn-taking, dominant personalities control the agenda. Fix: Enforce the democratic process with silent voting and equal speaking time.
5. No actions captured: Discussion without documented decisions wastes the conversation. Fix: Assign a dedicated note-taker capturing actions in real-time during each topic.
6. Skipping the vote: Facilitator decides the order instead of voting. Fix: This defeats the entire purpose. Always let the group vote and arrange by priority.
7. Parking lot graveyard: Undiscussed topics that are never revisited. Fix: Commit to carrying them forward. Review at the start of the next Lean Coffee retro.
</Gotchas>

<Instructions>
<Workflow - Lean Coffee Retro
  description="Facilitation guide for Lean Coffee retrospective."
>
1. [Setup] Create a Kanban board with three columns: "To Discuss," "Discussing," and "Discussed." Provide sticky notes, dot stickers, and a visible timer. Allocate time: 5 min topic generation, 3 min voting, remainder for discussion in 5-minute rounds.
2. [Open] (3 minutes)
   - Read the Prime Directive
   - Frame: "Today YOU set the agenda. There are no predetermined topics. Propose whatever is on your mind, the team will vote, and we'll discuss in priority order. Democracy in action."
   - Explain the process: "Write topics → Vote → Discuss in priority order → Vote to continue or move on."
3. [Stage: Topic Generation] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Let the team surface what actually matters to them
   - Prompt: "Write one topic per sticky note. Anything you want to discuss about our work, our process, our team, our sprint. No topic is off-limits. You don't need to have a solution, just something you want to talk about."
   - Facilitation tip: If people are shy, remind: "This can be a question, a concern, an idea, a celebration, or a problem. Anything."
4. [Stage: Brief Pitch] (3 minutes)
   - Purpose: Give everyone context on each proposed topic
   - Prompt: "Go around and give a 10-second pitch for each of your topics, just enough that people know what they're voting on."
   - Facilitation tip: Strict 10-second limit per pitch. No discussion yet, just context for voting.
5. [Stage: Dot-Vote] (2 minutes)
   - Purpose: Democratically prioritize the discussion agenda
   - Prompt: "You have 3 dots. Place them on the topics you most want to discuss. You can put multiple dots on one topic."
   - Facilitation tip: Count votes and arrange topics in priority order in the "To Discuss" column.
6. [Stage: Discuss (Repeating)] (5 minutes per topic)
   - Purpose: Focused discussion on each topic within a strict timebox
   - Prompt: Move the top topic to "Discussing." Say: "We have 5 minutes on this topic. [Topic proposer], give us 30 seconds of context, then open discussion."
   - Facilitation tip: Set a visible timer. When it rings, IMMEDIATELY call for a thumb vote.
7. [Stage: Continue/Move On Vote] (10 seconds)
   - Purpose: Let the group decide if a topic deserves more time
   - Prompt: "Thumbs up = continue this topic for 3 more minutes. Thumbs down = move to the next topic. Majority rules."
   - Facilitation tip: If continuing, give exactly 3 more minutes then move on regardless. Maximum one extension per topic.
8. [Stage: Capture Actions] (during discussion)
   - Purpose: Document decisions and action items as they emerge
   - Prompt: "What's the action here? Who owns it?"
   - Facilitation tip: Have a dedicated note-taker capturing actions in real-time as they surface during each topic.
9. [Synthesize] Move discussed topics to "Discussed" column. Capture any undiscussed topics as a "parking lot" for next session.
10. [Close] (3 minutes)
    - Read all action items captured
    - Acknowledge undiscussed topics: "These didn't make it today. We'll carry them forward."
    - Ask: "Did we discuss what mattered most?"
    - Thank the team for driving their own agenda
</Workflow - Lean Coffee Retro>
</Instructions>

<Resources>
- Your role is TIMEKEEPER, not topic-setter. Resist the urge to steer the conversation
- Strict adherence to time-boxes is the single most critical facilitation skill for this format
- If a topic generates huge energy but time is up, say: "This clearly matters. Let's schedule a dedicated 30-min session for this."
- For remote teams, use timer.guru or a shared screen timer. Everyone needs to see the countdown
- The "parking lot" is sacred. Review it at the start of the next Lean Coffee retro as carryover topics
- This format works best when rotated in occasionally (every 3-4 sprints) rather than used every time
- If engagement is low in your current format, switching to Lean Coffee is a powerful signal: "YOU decide what matters"
</Resources>
