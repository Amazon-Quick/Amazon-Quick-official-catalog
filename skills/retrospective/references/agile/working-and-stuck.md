# Working & Stuck

<Metadata>
Name: Working & Stuck
Category: agile
Aliases: Working/Not Working, What's Working/What's Stuck, Flow & Block
Duration: 15-25 minutes
Team Size: 3-7 (max 10)
Best For: Ultra-fast, present-tense check-in focused on immediate unblocking rather than historical reflection
Definition: The most minimalist two-column retrospective format, identifying what's currently working (flowing) and what's currently stuck (blocked), focused on present state rather than past events with the goal of resolving blockers in the meeting itself.
</Metadata>

<Gotchas>
1. Use when: Very limited time (15-20 minutes); team in the middle of intense delivery; previous retros produced too many unfinished action items; need immediate blockers resolved NOW; fast-moving teams, startup environments, pragmatic teams that dislike ceremony
2. Do NOT use when: Team needs celebration and recognition (no positive framing here); complex systemic issues need exploration; team health or morale assessment needed; historical reflection would provide valuable patterns
3. All Stuck, no Working: Team only sees problems, and hopelessness sets in. Fix: Push. Ask "What IS moving? Even something small."
4. Using it for months without variation: The simplicity becomes rote after 4-5 uses. Fix: Rotate to other formats periodically.
5. Stuck items without unblocking: If you identify blockers but don't resolve any in the meeting, this format has failed. Fix: The magic is resolving blockers IN the meeting. Make decisions on the spot.
6. Historical focus: "We were stuck last week." Fix: Redirect to present tense. Ask "What is stuck RIGHT NOW?"
7. No celebration of Working items: They exist to balance the picture. Fix: Don't skip them entirely. Acknowledge what's flowing even briefly.
</Gotchas>

<Instructions>
<Workflow - Working & Stuck
  description="Facilitation guide for Working & Stuck retrospective."
>
1. [Setup] Prepare a board with two columns: "Working ✅" and "Stuck 🚧." Keep it minimal. No fancy visuals needed. This format is about SPEED. Allocate time: 3 min brainstorm, 5 min share, 7 min unblock, 3 min close.
2. [Open] (1 minute)
   - No preamble needed. Frame: "Quick check-in. What's flowing right now? What's stuck right now? Present tense. Let's unblock what we can."
   - Note: This is present tense. Not "what went wrong last week" but "what is blocked RIGHT NOW."
3. [Stage: Silent Brainstorm] (3 minutes)
   - Purpose: Quick gut-check on current flow state
   - Prompt: "RIGHT NOW: what's WORKING? What's flowing smoothly, moving well, feeling good? And what's STUCK? What's blocked, stalled, not moving, frustrating you today?"
   - Facilitation tip: 3 minutes max. This format rewards instinct, not deep reflection.
4. [Stage: Share] (5 minutes)
   - Purpose: Surface current state quickly
   - Prompt: "Rapidfire. Place your notes, one sentence each. Working items first, then Stuck."
   - Facilitation tip: No long explanations. One sentence per item max. We're diagnosing, not storytelling.
5. [Stage: Immediate Unblocking] (7 minutes)
   - Purpose: Resolve blockers on the spot where possible
   - Prompt: "For each Stuck item: Can we unblock this RIGHT NOW? Who needs to talk to whom? What decision needs to be made? What can we resolve before leaving this room?"
   - Facilitation tip: This is the magic. Resolve what you can IN the meeting. Not everything needs an action item for next sprint. Some things just need a 30-second decision.
6. [Synthesize] Separate stuck items into: resolved now, needs a short action, and needs escalation. Working items need no action, just acknowledgment.
7. [Close] (2 minutes)
   - Read remaining unresolved items and assigned owners
   - Ask: "Are we less stuck than when we started?"
   - Done. Get back to work.
</Workflow - Working & Stuck>
</Instructions>

<Resources>
- This is your EMERGENCY format. 15 minutes, maximum impact, minimum ceremony
- The present-tense focus is the key differentiator. It's not "what went wrong" but "what IS stuck"
- Resolve blockers IN THE MEETING. This is not about documenting for later; it's about unblocking NOW
- Pairs perfectly with daily standups that surface but don't resolve issues. This is where resolution happens
- For remote teams, this works great as a 15-minute huddle
- Use bi-weekly as a quick check-in BETWEEN more thorough monthly retrospectives
- If a Stuck item requires a longer conversation, don't let it eat the whole meeting. Schedule a 1:1 and move on
- Consider standing for this retro (literally). It reinforces the brevity
</Resources>
